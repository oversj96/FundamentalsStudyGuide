Definitions and Theorems

For integers \(a\) and \(b\), define \(a\) divides \(b\).

For natural numbers \(a\) and \(b\) (or \(a\) is a divisor of \(b\), or \(b\) is a multiple of \(a\)) if and only if there is a natural number \(k\) such that \(b = ak\).

Define a proposition.

A proposition is a sentence that has exactly one truth value: true, which we denote by T, or false, which we denote by F.
